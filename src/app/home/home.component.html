The #MeToo phrase was first coined in 2006 by Tarana Burke, an advocate for women in New York. ... Then, in 2017, the phrase was reintroduced by actress Alyssa Milano as a way to encourage women and men to share their stories as part of an anti-sexual harassment movement.

During this time, The DIY punk scene was inspired; More abuse and injustice was called out in facebook groups, forums like deadformat, and google docs than ever before. However in 2021, there is no platform for the DIY community I could find where people can protect their communities.

This is a safe space where users can contribute anonymously and voice their concerns. I'm done with the bullshit. Predators and enablers? Nope, #NotInOurScene.

I have been in the music scene for many years and have had to endure the unfortunate power dynamics of being a part of a community that strives for inclusion but is not immune to it's wholly white, heteronormative, male dominated roots. I have had opportunities thrown at me throughout the years. The sad underlying theme of these being from people who had more power than myself, each one with certain implications or coercion, making me feel like my currency for success was not only musical talents, but my sex appeal and body. I have been assaulted in the music scene and seen friends, queer or non, affected.
